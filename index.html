About the System

Empowering citizens through transparent, efficient, and digital land governance.

This portal is developed under the National e-Governance Plan (NeGP) to digitise land dispute management across all Indian states and union territories.

Land litigation in India accounts for a significant share of all pending court cases. Delays in resolution often stem from manual, paper-based record-keeping and lack of real-time visibility for citizens and government officers alike.

The Digital Land Litigation Management System (DLLMS) addresses these challenges by providing a secure, unified platform connecting citizens, District Land Officers, Tahsildars, and the judiciary for end-to-end case management.

By digitising every stage — from case filing and document submission to hearing scheduling and final order issuance — DLLMS reduces processing time, eliminates physical visits, and ensures tamper-proof record maintenance.

System Objectives

Transparency

Every case update, document, and hearing order is logged and accessible to all authorised parties in real time.

Efficiency

Automated workflows reduce turnaround time by eliminating redundant manual processes and paper-based handling.

Digital Governance

Secure e-filing, e-signature support, and integration with national land record portals (DILRMP, eCourts).

Citizen-Centric Design

Multilingual interface, SMS/email notifications, and step-by-step guidance for every service.

Frequently Asked Questions
Required documents include: (1) Identity proof — Aadhaar Card or Voter ID; (2) Land documents — Patta, Chitta, FMB Sketch; (3) Sale deed / Gift deed / Will; (4) Encumbrance Certificate (EC); (5) Survey field map; (6) Any previous court order (if applicable). All documents must be self-attested and uploaded in PDF format.
Resolution time depends on the complexity of the case. Simple boundary disputes with clear documents may be resolved within 60–90 days. Complex title disputes or cases requiring field surveys may take 6–12 months. The DLLMS aims to reduce average resolution time by 40% compared to offline processing.
Filing a case on the DLLMS portal is free of charge. However, statutory court fees as prescribed under the applicable Revenue Act may apply at the hearing stage. These fees can be paid online via UPI, net banking, or debit card through the integrated payment gateway.

Legal Process & Timeline

Litigation follows a structured workflow under the Revenue Act. Estimated timelines vary based on case complexity.

Stage 1: Filing & Admission (1-7 Days)

Aadhaar-based filing, document verification by Revenue Inspector, and generation of Case ID.

Stage 2: Notice Issuance (15 Days)

Legal summons served to the respondent via registered post and digital dashboard.

Stage 3: Field Inspection (30 Days)

Surveyor conducts site visit for boundary/encroachment verification. Reports uploaded to DLLMS.

Stage 4: Hearing & Evidence (60-90 Days)

Statements recorded from both parties. Cross-verification of chitta/adangal records.

Stage 5: Final Order (90-120 Days)

Pronouncement of judgement by Tehsildar/Collector. Records updated in e-Patta system.

Key Terms Glossary
PattaPrimary ownership certificate for a specific piece of land.
Chitta/AdangalExtract of A-Register showing classification, tax, and crop details.
FMB / MapField Measurement Book extract showing sketch and boundaries.
EncumbranceHistory of past transactions, mortgages, or liens on the property.
PartitionLegal division of ancestral or joint property among legal heirs.
EvictionLegal removal of unauthorized occupants (Encroachers) from land.
RDORevenue Divisional Officer — Appellate authority for Tehsildar orders.

About DLLMS

The Digital Land Litigation Management System is an initiative under the National e-Governance Plan (NeGP), developed by the National Informatics Centre (NIC) for the Ministry of Land Records & Revenue, Government of India. The portal aims to digitise, streamline, and make transparent the entire land dispute resolution process across all Indian states and union territories.
